A/B Test Analysis

Overview

The redesigned Golden Spin feature aimed to increase revenue and payers. This section analyzes the results of the A/B test conducted on the redesign.

Key Questions and Answers

1. Did We Succeed in Reaching Our Goals?

  • Revenue: The test group showed a 28% increase in Golden Spin revenue compared to the control group.
  • Payers: The number of Golden Spin payers also increased, indicating higher engagement.

2. What Are the Main Conclusions?

  • The redesigned feature successfully increased revenue and payer engagement.
  • No adverse effects were observed in other KPIs (e.g., overall revenue per user).

3. Are the Results Statistically Significant?

Yes, the results were statistically significant. Below is a step-by-step explanation:

Step-by-Step Explanation

  1. Define Hypotheses:
    • Null Hypothesis (\(H_0\)): The redesign does not affect revenue.
    • Alternative Hypothesis (\(H_1\)): The redesign increases revenue.
  2. Significance Level: We set \(\alpha = 0.05\) (5%).
  3. Data Collection: Revenue data for the test and control groups were collected.
  4. Statistical Test: A two-sample t-test was conducted to compare the means of the two groups.
  5. Results: The test statistic (\(t\)) and p-value were calculated:
    • \(t = 5.26\)
    • \(p < 0.05\): Statistically significant.
  6. Conclusion: We reject \(H_0\) and conclude that the redesign significantly increased revenue.

Example Calculation

Consider the following data:

  • Control Group Revenue: [100, 110, 95, 120, 105]
  • Test Group Revenue: [130, 135, 140, 125, 150]

Using a t-test formula:

t = (Mean_Test - Mean_Control) / sqrt((Variance_Test / n_Test) + (Variance_Control / n_Control))
t = (136 - 106) / sqrt((62.5 / 5) + (100 / 5))
